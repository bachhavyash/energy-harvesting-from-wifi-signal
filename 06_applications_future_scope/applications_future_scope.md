# Applications and Future Scope

## Applications
Energy harvesting from ambient Wi-Fi signals can be applied in several
low-power and battery-sensitive scenarios:

- **IoT Sensors**  
  Suitable for low-power sensing applications in smart homes,
  agriculture, and industrial monitoring.

- **Wireless Sensor Networks**  
  Enables long-term deployment without frequent battery replacement.

- **Smart Home Devices**  
  Powering devices such as motion detectors, temperature sensors,
  and low-energy controllers.

- **Remote Monitoring Systems**  
  Useful in locations where battery maintenance is difficult or costly.

---

## Advantages
- Eliminates or reduces dependency on batteries
- Environment-friendly and sustainable energy source
- Low maintenance operation
- Compact and scalable design
- Continuous availability of ambient RF energy

---

## Limitations
- Very low harvested power levels
- Short effective harvesting range
- Strong dependence on Wi-Fi availability
- Complex antenna tuning and impedance matching
- Not suitable for high-power applications

---

## Future Scope
The project can be extended in several directions:

- Multi-band RF energy harvesting (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular)
- Hybrid energy harvesting (RF + solar or vibration)
- Improved antenna and rectifier efficiency
- Use of ultra-low-power microcontrollers and sensors
- Intelligent energy management algorithms

---

## Summary
Energy harvesting from Wi-Fi signals presents a promising solution
for sustainable and battery-free low-power systems. While current
limitations exist, future advancements can significantly improve
efficiency and applicability.
